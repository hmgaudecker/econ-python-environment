EditorsÂ¶

A good editor is the Swiss Army knife of any serious programmer: It can do almost anything and makes you much more productive. The built-in ones are not an option.

I personally use Sublime Text. If you are reading this because you are about to attend my Effective Programming Practices for Economists course, I suggest you to try it out for the time being. It is free to try out for a while and it will make it easier for you to follow my live coding.

Modern open source alternatives are Light Table or Atom, Emacs and Vim are Unix classics that will stay around among power users. They are certainly not for the faint of heart, though.
